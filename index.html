Project Goals

The goal of this project is to analyze adult arrest data across a network of New York State counties to identify relationships between arrest types, geography, time, and several other factors. Specifically, we aim to explore how felony and misdemeanor arrest rates have changed over the years, whether certain counties show disproportionately high rates of drug or violent felonies, and what factors may contribute to these disparities. By examining the breakdown and patterns related to arrest types (both felonies and misdemeanors), we hope to gain a clearer picture of how criminal justice trends vary across New York state. Ultimately, this analysis could provide useful insight into broader social and economic questions surrounding crime.

Exploratory Data Analysis

Below we compute 5 summary statistics to begin understanding the shape and trends in the data, followed by a visualization of statewide arrest trends over time.

# --- Summary Statistics --- # 1. Total arrests across all counties and years print("1. Total arrests in dataset:", df['Total'].sum()) # 2. Average total arrests per county per year print("2. Avg arrests per county/year:", round(df['Total'].mean(), 1)) # 3. County with highest all-time total arrests top_county = df.groupby('County')['Total'].sum().idxmax() top_val = df.groupby('County')['Total'].sum().max() print(f"3. County with most total arrests: {top_county} ({top_val:,})") # 4. Year with highest statewide arrests yearly = df.groupby('Year')['Total'].sum() peak_year = yearly.idxmax() print(f"4. Year with most statewide arrests: {peak_year} ({yearly[peak_year]:,})") # 5. Felony vs misdemeanor split (statewide average %) felony_pct = (df['Felony Total'].sum() / df['Total'].sum()) * 100 misd_pct = (df['Misdemeanor Total'].sum() / df['Total'].sum()) * 100 print(f"5. Statewide felony rate: {felony_pct:.1f}% | misdemeanor rate: {misd_pct:.1f}%")

These five statistics give us a foundational understanding of the dataset. The county and year peaks will guide our deeper geographic and temporal analysis going forward.

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt # Line chart: statewide total arrests by year yearly_totals = df.groupby('Year')['Total'].sum() plt.figure(figsize=(12, 5)) plt.plot(yearly_totals.index, yearly_totals.values, color='steelblue', linewidth=2) plt.title('Total Adult Arrests in New York State by Year (1970–2024)', fontsize=14) plt.xlabel('Year') plt.ylabel('Total Arrests') plt.grid(True, alpha=0.3) plt.tight_layout() plt.show()

Figure 1: This line chart shows the trend in total adult arrests across all New York counties from 1970 to 2024. The graph reveals peaks and declines that may correspond to shifts in policing policy, population changes, or broader social trends — all of which we plan to investigate further.
